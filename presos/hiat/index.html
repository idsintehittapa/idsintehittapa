Period poverty is the term used to describe a lack of access to menstrual hygiene products and education, toilets, hand washing facilities, and/or waste management

globalcitizen.org

Even in developed countries, such as Sweden, period poverty exists and is a persistent problem!

On average, menstruators endure 55 000 hours of their lives menstruating, use 10 000 hygiene products, and spend 63 000 SEK

There are several implications that arise from period poverty:

  • Staying home from school and miss out on education

  • Using unhygienic and ineffective alternative methods (toilet paper, rags, etc.)

  • Experiencing shame, stress, and discomfort
